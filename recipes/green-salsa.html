Tomatillo Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 tomatillos
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • Serrano Pepper or Jalapeno Pepper
  • Cilantro (6 to 8 sprigs)
  • 1/2 white onion
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Spread the tomatillos, unpeeled garlic, and chiles on a rimmed baking sheet.
  2. Roast the ingredients under a broiler, about 4 inches below the heat source.
    • Roast until the tomatillos and chiles are blotchy brown on one side, approximately 6 minutes.
    • Flip everything over and roast the other side.
    • The garlic should feel soft.
  3. Allow the roasted ingredients to cool until handleable.
  4. Peel the garlic cloves and scrape everything, including any juices, into a blender or food processor.
  5. Pulse the ingredients in the blender or food processor until you achieve the desired consistency (smooth but with some texture).
  6. Gather the cilantro into a tight bundle and thinly slice across it, including leaves and stems, until you run out of leaves.
  7. Chop the onion into approximately Â¼-inch pieces.
    • Rinse the chopped onion in a strainer under cold water to remove the strong flavor.
    • Shake off excess water and add the onion to the salsa.
  8. Add the sliced cilantro to the salsa.
  9. If the salsa is too thick, stir in water a tablespoon at a time until it reaches the desired consistency.
  10. Taste the salsa and season with salt, typically around a scant teaspoon.
    • Note: Adjusting other ingredients may be difficult due to the roasted nature of the salsa, except for cilantro and onion.
